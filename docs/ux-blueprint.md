# UX Blueprint

## Experience Direction

The storefront should feel premium, editorial, and category-intelligent rather than generic marketplace UI. The goal is not maximal visual density. It is confident merchandising, strong contrast, clear browsing hierarchy, and expressive typography paired with crisp ecommerce mechanics.

The visual tone should sit between a design-forward journal brand and a modern retail storefront. Users should feel that products are curated, useful, and trustworthy rather than dumped into a giant catalog.

## Visual Pillars

1. Bold typography with a high-contrast headline system.
2. Warm, material-inspired color palette instead of default tech blues or purple gradients.
3. Large product cards and editorial feature panels for hero zones.
4. Clean surfaces with precise spacing and a restrained amount of motion.
5. Strong merchandising hierarchy across campaigns, categories, and product recommendations.

## Brand System

### Typography

1. Heading font direction: Fraunces or Cormorant Infant for editorial emphasis.
2. Body and UI font direction: Space Grotesk or Plus Jakarta Sans for modern clarity.
3. Product pricing and metadata should use a compact UI style with strong weight contrast.

### Color Tokens

1. Ink: #1E1A17 for primary text and key UI anchors.
2. Paper: #F5EFE6 for large page surfaces.
3. Canvas: #FFFDFC for cards and elevated content blocks.
4. Forest: #335C4B for trust accents, confirmations, and category depth.
5. Terracotta: #B85C38 for promotional highlights and callouts.
6. Mustard: #D4A43C for seasonal accents and rating or offer details.
7. Graphite: #4E4A46 for secondary text.
8. Mist: #DDD3C7 for borders and subtle dividers.

### Motion Principles

1. Page entry should use subtle staggered reveals rather than flashy transitions.
2. Hover motion should clarify affordances, not entertain.
3. Filters, drawer panels, and mini-cart transitions should feel quick and tactile.
4. Recommendation and campaign modules can use light horizontal movement cues.

## Layout Principles

1. Mobile-first page architecture.
2. Above-the-fold content must establish category confidence immediately.
3. Primary actions should remain visually dominant and stable.
4. Filters should be easy to scan and fast to dismiss on mobile.
5. Long pages should use section rhythm, alternating dense and spacious zones.

## Key Screens

### Home Page

Purpose: Introduce the brand, surface seasonal demand, route users into categories, and drive first-click confidence.

Sections:
1. Premium hero with campaign headline, supporting copy, and category-led CTA.
2. Fast-entry category grid for notebooks, pens, art supplies, office essentials, files and folders, desk accessories, and school kits.
3. Merchandising rails such as Bestsellers, Back to School, Exam Essentials, Study Desk Setup, and Corporate Bulk Picks.
4. Trust rail with delivery, GST invoice, secure payment, and support signals.
5. Recommendation block informed by popular products or browsing context.

### Listing Page

Purpose: Help users narrow quickly without overwhelming them.

Requirements:
1. Strong breadcrumb and category context.
2. Sticky or easy-access filters on mobile.
3. Filters for price, brand, rating, stock, color, pack size, page type, nib size, and use case where relevant.
4. Sort for popularity, price, newest, discount, and rating.
5. Product cards with clear price, offer state, stock state, wishlist action, and quick variant preview when practical.

### Product Detail Page

Purpose: Remove doubt and support both quick purchase and considered purchase.

Requirements:
1. Large, swipe-friendly product gallery.
2. Clear product title, brand, rating summary, and price block.
3. Variant selector with obvious stock feedback.
4. Delivery estimate and trust messaging.
5. Bulk inquiry or bulk pricing nudge where relevant.
6. Product details grouped into Overview, Specifications, Shipping, and Invoice Eligibility.
7. Related and complementary products.

### Cart And Checkout

Purpose: Minimize friction while maintaining pricing clarity.

Requirements:
1. Login gate before purchase starts.
2. Cart supports quantity changes, inventory feedback, coupon entry, and savings display.
3. Checkout stages: login confirmation, address, delivery summary, payment, review.
4. UPI should be visually prioritized for Indian mobile users.
5. COD eligibility must be clear before final payment step.

### Account Area

Purpose: Give users a practical post-purchase hub.

Requirements:
1. Orders dashboard with visible statuses and reorder shortcuts.
2. Saved addresses.
3. Wishlist.
4. Profile and support actions.
5. Bulk order requests and responses if enabled for the user.

## Component Inventory

1. Header with search, category access, account, wishlist, and cart.
2. Predictive search panel.
3. Mega menu or mobile category drawer.
4. Product card and collection card.
5. Filter drawer and applied-filter chips.
6. Pricing module with discount logic display.
7. Quantity stepper.
8. Variant selector.
9. Recommendation rail.
10. Bulk inquiry card.
11. Coupon module.
12. Delivery promise module.
13. Support entry module.
14. Order status timeline.
15. GST invoice badge or info module.

## UX Guardrails

1. Avoid visual clutter that imitates general marketplaces.
2. Do not hide essential information behind excessive tabs.
3. Keep campaign styling within the core brand palette.
4. Preserve readability and tap target quality on mobile.
5. Use recommendation modules to support discovery, not to hijack the primary task.
